The Big Picture — 5 Zones of the Dashboard

Each section of the UI tells you something different

① Stats Bar — Today's Scorecard

How many buy/sell signals fired today, win rate, total P&L in points, and Heikin-Ashi trend streak. It's the "at a glance" summary.

② Active Trade Banner — Live Position

Shows your current open trade — which direction (long/short), entry price, stop loss, profit target, and live running P&L.

③ The Price Chart — Price Action

Candlestick chart with BUY/SELL arrows, Support/Resistance lines, and the SuperTrend indicator line. This is where all decisions come from.

④ Right-Side Level Labels

Live price labels floating next to the chart: R (resistance), S (support), ENTRY, STOP LOSS, and SuperTrend. These update tick by tick.

⑤ Market Mood Panel (top-right) — Sentiment

AI-powered news scanner reads live articles and classifies them as BULLISH, BEARISH, or NEUTRAL. The bar + score gives you a quick read on what the broader market is feeling.

Reading Candlestick Charts

Each candle = one time period (1 min, 5 min, 15 min, or 1 Day)

🟢 Green Candle — Price Went UP

Also called a Bullish candle. Buyers controlled this period.

← Highest price reached (top wick)
← Green body: opened low, closed HIGH
← Lowest price before recovering

🔴 Red Candle — Price Went DOWN

Also called a Bearish candle. Sellers controlled this period.

← Highest price before falling (top wick)
← Red body: opened high, closed LOW
← Lowest price reached (bottom wick)

Time Frame Buttons: 1m | 5m | 15m | 1D

5m = each candle covers 5 minutes. 1D = one candle per full trading day. For intraday options trading, use 5m — best balance of speed and noise.

Heikin-Ashi (HA) Mode

Smoothed candles that filter out noise. When the bot shows "HA: ▼ 8 TREND", it means 8 consecutive red HA candles = strong downtrend. ▲ = uptrend streak. These are cleaner signals than raw candles.

Signal Markers — What the Arrows Mean

The bot places these on the chart automatically based on AI + indicator analysis

▲ BUY Signal (green arrow)

Appears below a candle pointing UP. The bot thinks price will rise. If you trade, buy a Call (CE) option. Best when SuperTrend is also green.

Stronger when: RSI rising + HA bullish + price near Support

▼ SELL Signal (red arrow)

Appears above a candle pointing DOWN. The bot thinks price will fall. If you trade, buy a Put (PE) option. Best when SuperTrend is also red.

Stronger when: RSI falling + HA bearish + price near Resistance

⭐ STRONG BUY / STRONG SELL

Multiple indicators all agree at the same moment. These are the highest-confidence signals. Shown as "X Strong" in the stats bar. Prioritise these over regular signals.

Rule of thumb: only trade STRONG signals until you're comfortable

🔶 SL × (orange cross)

Stop Loss hit. The trade was automatically closed because price moved against the position too far. The percentage shown is how much was lost on that trade.

🟢 EXIT (green dot)

Profit taken. The bot exited because the target was reached or the signal reversed favourably. The percentage shown is the profit earned on that trade.

● TRACKING (cyan dot)

A small cyan/blue dot at the current price means the bot is actively monitoring this position in real-time and will auto-exit at SL or Target.

The Active Trade Banner — Your Live Position

Appears when a trade is open — the purple bar across the top of the chart area

ACTIVE SHORT ▼ | Entry: 24175.60 | SL: 24205.03 | Target: 24104.28 | P&L: -0.55 pts ● TRACKING

ACTIVE SHORT ▼ — Direction

SHORT = you are betting price goes DOWN. To play this, buy a PUT (PE) option.

ACTIVE LONG ▲ = betting price goes UP → buy a CALL (CE) option.

Entry: 24175.60 — Where You Got In

The Nifty index price when the trade was opened. Shown as a blue horizontal line on the chart labelled "ENTRY ▼". This is your reference point.

SL: 24205.03 — Stop Loss

If Nifty rises to 24205, the trade closes automatically to limit losses. For a SHORT trade, SL is above entry. For a LONG trade, SL is below entry. Shown as orange "STOP LOSS" label on the chart.

Target: 24104.28 — Profit Exit

If Nifty falls to 24104, the trade closes with profit. Risk = 30 pts (to SL) vs Reward = 71 pts (to Target) → Risk:Reward of 1:2.4 — very good!

Always check that SL:Target ratio is at least 1:1.5 before entering. A ratio below 1:1 is unfavourable — you risk more than you can gain.

Support (S) and Resistance (R) — Price Floors & Ceilings

Horizontal price levels where the market tends to react

🔴 R = Resistance — the Ceiling

A price level where sellers historically step in and push price back down. When price approaches R 78383, expect rejection or slowdown.

IF price breaks cleanly above R → very strong BUY signal. The ceiling became a floor.

🟢 S = Support — the Floor

A price level where buyers historically step in and push price back up. When price approaches S 77726, expect a bounce.

IF price breaks cleanly below S → very strong SELL signal. The floor became a ceiling.

Best entries: buy near S, sell near R. Never enter in the middle — no edge there.

SuperTrend — The Trend Direction Indicator

The single most important line on the chart — always check this first

🟢 Green line BELOW price = Uptrend

The green SuperTrend line acts as a moving support floor. Price bounces up off it. As long as candles stay above the green line → trend is UP.

Action: Only take BUY / CE trades. Ignore SELL signals.

If price dips to the green line and then bounces up with a BUY signal = high-probability entry

🔴 Red line ABOVE price = Downtrend

The red SuperTrend line acts as a moving resistance ceiling. Price gets rejected down from it. As long as candles stay below the red line → trend is DOWN.

Action: Only take SELL / PE trades. Ignore BUY signals.

If price rallies to the red line and then falls with a SELL signal = high-probability entry

⚡ "What's that dramatic spike/dip?" — The Flip

When you see the SuperTrend line make a sudden dramatic spike down (or up), it means the trend just flipped direction.


Before flip: Red line ABOVE price (downtrend)

▌▌▌ price candles ▌▌▌
↕ FLIP (spike you see)
▌▌▌ price candles ▌▌▌

After flip: Green line BELOW price (uptrend)

The chart connects both positions with a vertical line, creating the dramatic spike you see. It is not a data error — it's the most important event on the chart.

A SuperTrend flip = major trend change. Best to wait 1–2 candles after the flip to confirm before entering a trade in the new direction.

How to use Market Mood in trading

Use it as a filter, not a trigger. It helps you skip bad trades:


BUY signal + BULLISH mood ✅ Strong setup
SELL signal + BEARISH mood ✅ Strong setup
BUY signal + BEARISH mood ⚠️ Be cautious
SELL signal + BULLISH mood ⚠️ Be cautious
Market mood is based on news that can be hours old. Always prioritise live price action (chart signals) over sentiment. Sentiment = context, not trigger.

How to Make a Trade — Step by Step

Follow this checklist every time before entering any position

  • 1

    Check SuperTrend Color

    Green below price → uptrend, only take BUY signals. Red above price → downtrend, only take SELL signals. Never trade against SuperTrend.

  • 2

    Wait for a STRONG Signal

    Look for "STRONG BUY ▲" or "STRONG SELL ▼". Regular signals are lower confidence. STRONG = multiple indicators all agree simultaneously.

  • 3

    Confirm with Market Mood

    BUY + BULLISH mood = confident. SELL + BEARISH mood = confident. Mismatches = sit out or reduce position size.

  • 4

    Check Where Price Is vs S/R

    Buying near a Support level is safer than buying in the middle of a range. Selling near Resistance is safer than selling mid-range.

  • 5

    Note Entry, SL, and Target

    Read the Active Trade Banner. Calculate your risk:reward. Only proceed if Target is at least 1.5× the distance to SL.

  • 6

    Use the Budget Optimizer

    Enter your capital in the "Smart Budget Optimizer" panel → click "Find Best Option" → the bot recommends the ideal strike price and lot size for your budget.

  • 7

    BUY signal → buy CE option
    SELL signal → buy PE option

    CE = Call = profits when index goes UP. PE = Put = profits when index goes DOWN. Always buy options, never sell (write) them as a beginner.

  • 8

    Let the Bot Manage the Exit

    Don't manually close too early. The bot tracks SL and Target automatically. Review results in Trade History to learn which setups work best.
